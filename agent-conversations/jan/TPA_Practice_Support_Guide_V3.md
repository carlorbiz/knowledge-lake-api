# TPA Practice Support Guide
## Version 3.0 (2026)

**Purpose:** This framework provides structure and guidance to the Regional Team when engaging with Training Posts and Supervisors. The role of the Training Program Advisor (TPA) is to be the College's point of contact for Training Posts and Supervisors at all stages of training an ACRRM registrar.

**Version:** 3.0  
**Effective Date:** January 2026  
**Replaces:** Version 2.0

---

## Training Post Support Touchpoints Overview

### Before Engaging with a Registrar

#### Initial Accreditation

**TPA Role:**
- Support the practice to provide required evidence for the accreditation process, including the Training Post Agreement
- Provide links to support resources
- Conduct site visit (may be virtual) as required by the Post Accreditation Team

**Key Actions:**
- Introduce TPA as point of contact for ACRRM
- Explain ACRRM training pathways and funding mechanisms
- Assist with Training Post Agreement completion
- Provide accreditation checklist and required documentation list
- Schedule site visit (virtual or in-person) with Post Accreditation Team

---

#### Re-accreditation

**TPA Role:**
- Support the practice to provide required evidence for re-accreditation (as directed by Post Accreditation team)
- Review and update Training Post Agreement
- Conduct site visit if required

**Key Actions:**
- Monitor accreditation expiry dates in RGHub
- Contact training post at least 6 months before accreditation expiry
- Provide updated accreditation checklist and required documentation
- Assist with Training Post Agreement review and updates
- Coordinate site visit with Post Accreditation Team if required

---

### Onboarding a Newly Accredited Training Post

**TPA Role:**
- Introduce TPA as point of contact for ACRRM
- Explain ACRRM training pathways and funding mechanisms
- Ensure Practice Manager has RGHub login details and familiarisation
- Create web user details in Aptify
- Verify funding access setup
- Explain placement process and capacity collection

**Key Actions:**

**1. Introduction and Role Clarification**
- Introduce TPA role: administrative and practice support throughout training lifecycle
- Explain regional team structure (TPA, Medical Educator, RDOT, SLO)
- Provide contact details and communication preferences

**2. ACRRM Training Pathways**
- Explain AGPT, RGTS, IP, RVTS pathways
- Clarify eligibility criteria and funding mechanisms for each pathway
- Provide pathway comparison table from ACRRM Practice Support Handbook

**3. RGHub Access and Familiarisation**
- Confirm Practice Manager has "web user" access to RGHub
- Provide RGHub login details and password reset instructions
- Guide Practice Manager through RGHub navigation (Training Post Profile, placement management, reporting)
- Explain how to update contact details and training post capacity

**4. Aptify Web User Creation**
- Create web user details in Aptify for Practice Manager
- Explain that all TPA communications and support will be logged in Aptify
- Provide Aptify login details (if Practice Manager will have access)

**5. Funding Access Setup**

**For AGPT Registrars:**
- Confirm Practice Manager has active **PRODA credentials**
- Verify PRODA account linked to training post's **Organisation Site ID**
- Confirm **HPOS access is functional** (test and verify)
- Required for:
  - National Consistent Payments (NCP) during CGT
  - Flexible Funds during AST

**For RGTS Registrars (From Semester 1, 2026):**

**RGTS Funding Transition:**

From Semester 1, 2026, RGTS registrars access **GP Training Incentive Payments** via PRODA/HPOS (same process as AGPT). TPAs must ensure RGTS posts have active PRODA/HPOS access from Semester 1, 2026 onwards.

**Action for TPA:**
- Confirm Practice Manager has:
  - Active PRODA credentials
  - HPOS access linked to Organisation Site ID
  - Understanding of GP Training Incentive Payments claims process

**Note:** Bank account details in RGHub Training Post Profile are no longer required for RGTS funding from Semester 1, 2026.

**6. Placement Process Overview**
- Explain registrar placement workflow (allocation list, regional team meeting, placement approval, placement documentation)
- Clarify training post's role in placement process
- Explain capacity collection process and how capacity affects placement allocation

**7. Training Post Profile Completion**
- Assist Practice Manager to complete Training Post Profile in RGHub
- Verify Organisation Site ID is recorded in Aptify and RGHub
- Confirm contact details (Practice Manager, supervisors, practice address, phone, email)
- Update practice capacity (number of registrars post can accommodate)

### Preparing for the Commencement of a Registrar

**TPA Role:**
- Review placement documentation and ensure completeness
- Confirm employment contract compliance
- Verify MPN application submitted
- Confirm funding access is functional

**Key Actions:**

**1. Placement Documentation**
- Confirm Placement Confirmation Form completed and submitted
- Verify Training Post Agreement signed and returned to ACRRM
- Ensure placement details correct in RGHub (registrar name, start date, training pathway, term level)

**2. Employment Contract Compliance**

**All AGPT and RGTS Registrars:**

TPAs must confirm Practice Manager is using:

- **GPSA/GPRA employment contract templates** (mandatory for AGPT and RGTS)
- Contracts compliant with **NTCER Version 2025-02** (effective from pay cycle following 1 July 2025)

**Key Provisions to Verify:**
- **12% Superannuation Guarantee** (from 1 July 2025, paid with salary from 1 July 2026)
- **Monthly minimum billing cycle** (no longer fortnightly or weekly)
- **3.5% staged increase for CGT1 registrars** (commencing Semester 1, 2026)
- Mandated formal teaching time (3 hours/week GPT1, 1.5 hours/week GPT2)
- Educational release time (16 days per 12 months)
- Leave entitlements (annual leave, personal/carer's leave, parental leave, study leave)

**For Aboriginal Medical Services (AMS):**
- Request **copy of employment agreement before registrar commences**
- Required to enable AMS Salary Support payment processing
- Employment Agreement must use GPSA/GPRA template and comply with NTCER Version 2025-02

**Resources:**
- GPSA Employment Contracts: [https://gpsa.org.au/training-practice-menu/employment-support/](https://gpsa.org.au/training-practice-menu/employment-support/)
- NTCER 2025-02: [https://gpra.org.au/ntcer/](https://gpra.org.au/ntcer/)

**3. Medicare Provider Number (MPN) Application**
- Confirm registrar has submitted MPN application via MyCollege Dashboard **at least 8 weeks prior to commencement**
- Verify College has endorsed MPN application and forwarded to Medicare
- Follow up if MPN not issued by commencement date
- **Multi-site placements:** Confirm separate MPN applications submitted for each accredited location
- Remind Practice Manager: Patients cannot claim Medicare rebate if registrar does not have active MPN

**4. Funding Access Verification**

**For AGPT Registrars:**
- Confirm Practice Manager has tested PRODA/HPOS access and can access NCP claims portal
- Verify Organisation Site ID is correctly linked in PRODA

**For RGTS Registrars (From Semester 1, 2026):**
- Confirm Practice Manager has tested PRODA/HPOS access and understands GP Training Incentive Payments claims process
- Verify Organisation Site ID is correctly linked in PRODA

**If AMS:**
- Confirm copy of employment agreement received by TPA
- Escalate to ACRRM Finance Team to enable AMS Salary Support processing

**5. Orientation Planning**
- Remind Practice Manager to prepare orientation pack (practice information, staff contacts, community information, key policies)
- Confirm orientation schedule for registrar's first week
- Verify rostering includes quarantined formal teaching time (3 hours/week GPT1, 1.5 hours/week GPT2)

---

### Early Semester Check-in (Week 5)

**TPA Role:**
- Conduct Core Generalist Training (CGT) Pulse Check
- Confirm registrar settling well and orientation complete
- Verify formal teaching time quarantined in roster
- Remind Practice Manager of mid-semester reporting requirements

**Key Actions:**

Use the **Week 5 CGT Training Post Check Template** (see separate document) to guide conversation with Practice Manager.

**Core Questions to Address:**
- Has registrar commenced and settled well?
- Was orientation completed (practice orientation and community integration)?
- Is formal teaching time quarantined in roster (3 hours/week GPT1, 1.5 hours/week GPT2)?
- Are supervision arrangements working effectively?
- Is MPN active and registrar billing Medicare?
- Is employment contract compliant with NTCER Version 2025-02?
- Does practice have active PRODA/HPOS access (if AGPT or RGTS from S1 2026)?
- Any concerns or support needs?

**Follow-up Actions:**
- Document contact in Aptify as Contact Log entry
- Escalate any concerns to Medical Educator or RDOT as appropriate
- Remind Practice Manager that mid-semester report will be due at Week 11

---

### Mid-Semester Reporting (Week 11)

**TPA Role:**
- Ensure Practice Manager completes and submits Mid-Semester Training Practice Report
- Follow up if report not received

**Key Actions:**
- ACRRM sends Electronic Direct Mail (EDM) with link to Mid-Semester Training Practice Report (Microsoft Forms)
- TPA monitors report submission status in RGHub
- If report not received by due date, contact Practice Manager to follow up
- Escalate to Medical Educator if report indicates concerns about registrar progression or training environment

---

### End-of-Semester Reporting (Week 22 onwards)

**TPA Role:**
- Ensure Practice Manager completes and submits End-of-Semester Training Practice Report
- Assist Practice Manager to support Principal Supervisor with Supervisor Report completion
- Follow up if reports not received

**Key Actions:**
- ACRRM sends EDM with link to End-of-Semester Training Practice Report (MS Forms)
- TPA monitors report submission status in RGHub
- Remind Practice Manager to assist Principal Supervisor with Supervisor Report completion (currently paper-based)
- Provide Supervisor Report template link if needed: [Supervisor Report Template](https://www.acrrm.org.au/docs/default-source/all-files/supervisor-report-template.docx)
- If reports not received by due date, contact Practice Manager to follow up
- Escalate to Medical Educator if reports indicate concerns

### Ongoing Support of the Training Post (Throughout Semester)

**TPA Role:**
- Provide ongoing administrative and practice support throughout semester
- Monitor training post compliance with ACRRM Standards and NTCER requirements
- Provide policy updates and funding guidance
- Respond to queries and troubleshoot issues
- Maintain regular contact to build strong, supportive relationships

**Key Actions:**

**1. Policy and Funding Updates**

TPAs should inform training posts about:

**GP Training Incentive Payments (Effective Semester 1, 2026):**
- **$30,000 Salary Support** per registrar per annum (paid to training post/employer)
- **5 paid study leave days** per year (in addition to educational release time)
- **Up to 20 weeks paid parental leave** for primary caregivers
- Accessed via PRODA/HPOS (RGTS registrars from Semester 1, 2026)

**NTCER Version 2025-02 Key Changes:**
- **Monthly minimum billing cycle** (no longer fortnightly or weekly)
- **12% Superannuation Guarantee** from 1 July 2025 (paid with salary from 1 July 2026)
- **3.5% CGT1 base rate increase** (registrars commencing Semester 1, 2026)

**Flexible Funds (AGPT AST Registrars):**
- **Quarterly payments to training posts** for clinical teaching during AST terms
- Practice Managers should **confirm payment receipt each quarter**
- Report discrepancies to TPA for escalation to ACRRM Finance Team

**2. Responding to Queries**

Common queries TPAs may receive:
- Funding access issues (PRODA/HPOS setup, payment delays, NCP claims)
- Employment contract questions (NTCER compliance, salary rates, leave entitlements)
- MPN application delays or multi-site coverage questions
- Reporting requirements and deadlines
- Accreditation renewal process and timelines
- Supervisor professional development requirements

**Escalation Pathways:**
- **ACRRM Finance Team:** Persistent funding payment issues, PRODA/HPOS technical problems
- **Post Accreditation Team:** Accreditation compliance concerns, re-accreditation queries
- **Medical Educator:** Registrar progression concerns, educational issues, assessment queries
- **National AST Coordinator:** AST-specific clinical or educational queries (not administrative)
- **RDOT:** Serious concerns affecting registrar safety, training quality, or workplace issues

**3. Regular Contact and Relationship Building**
- Maintain regular contact throughout semester (not just when problems arise)
- Proactive check-ins to identify emerging issues early
- Recognition and appreciation for training post's contribution to rural medical education
- Document all contacts in Aptify as Contact Log entries

---

## Mandatory Contact Logging in Aptify

**Each contact with the Training Post and/or Supervisor must be recorded in Aptify as a Contact Log entry.**

This ensures:
- Continuity of support and quality assurance
- Other TPAs can understand the post's history and support needs if TPA changes
- ACRRM can monitor training post engagement and identify support needs

**Best Practice:**
- Log contact immediately after each interaction (phone call, email, site visit, check-in)
- Include sufficient detail for another TPA to understand context
- Use consistent categories and tags for easy searching
- Record outcomes and follow-up actions required

## Special Considerations

### Advanced Specialised Training (AST) Posts

**TPA Support for AST Posts:**

**Administrative Support (TPA Responsibility):**
- Accreditation processes and documentation
- Placement documentation and reporting
- Funding access and payment queries (Flexible Funds for AGPT AST registrars)
- General training post administrative support

**Clinical and Educational Support (National AST Coordinator Responsibility):**
- AST-specific curriculum and learning objectives
- AST assessment requirements and processes
- Registrar progression and educational planning in AST disciplines
- Discipline-specific training issues and requirements

**Contact Pathways:**
- **Administrative queries:** Contact regional TPA
- **AST-specific educational and clinical queries:** Escalate to National AST Coordinator (via [practicesupport@acrrm.org.au](mailto:practicesupport@acrrm.org.au))

---

### Aboriginal Medical Services (AMS)

**AMS-Specific Requirements:**

**AMS Salary Support:**
- AMS employing AGPT registrars are eligible for AMS Salary Support payments
- **Requirement:** AMS must provide copy of employment agreement to TPA before registrar commences
- Employment Agreement must use GPSA/GPRA template and comply with NTCER Version 2025-02
- TPA forwards employment agreement to ACRRM Finance Team to enable Salary Support processing

**Cultural Safety:**
- TPAs should be aware of cultural safety requirements for AMS placements
- Support AMS to provide culturally safe learning environments for all registrars
- Connect AMS with ACRRM resources on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Health training

---

### Hospital and Secondary Care Placements

**Hospital CGT Placements:**
- Hospitals accredited for CGT must provide primary care clinics, emergency department experience, and inpatient care
- Supervision by accredited ACRRM supervisors or hospital specialists with ACRRM supervisor accreditation
- TPAs provide same support as for general practice placements (onboarding, check-ins, reporting, funding access)

**Hospitalist Training:**
- ACRRM recognises Hospitalist training as component of Rural Generalist training
- TPAs should be familiar with Hospitalist training requirements and resources
