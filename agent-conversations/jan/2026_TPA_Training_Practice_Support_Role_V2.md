## Slide 4: Training Post Support Touchpoints Overview

**Your role as TPA is to be the College's point of contact for Training Posts and Supervisors at all stages of training an ACRRM registrar.**

### Before Engaging with a Registrar

**Initial Accreditation**
- Support the practice to provide required evidence for the accreditation process
- Assist with Training Post Agreement completion
- Provide links to support resources
- Conduct site visit (may be virtual) as required by Post Accreditation Team

**Re-accreditation**
- Support the practice to provide required evidence for re-accreditation (as directed by Post Accreditation team)
- Review and update Training Post Agreement
- Conduct site visit if required

## Slide 5: Training Post Support Touchpoints (Continued)

### During Registrar Training

**Onboarding**
- Introduce TPA as point of contact for ACRRM
- Explain ACRRM training pathways and funding mechanisms
- Ensure Practice Manager has RGHub login details and familiarisation
- Create web user details in Aptify
- Verify funding access setup (PRODA/HPOS or bank details)
- Explain placement process and capacity collection

**Preparing for Commencement**
- Review placement documentation and ensure completeness
- Confirm employment contract compliance (GPSA/GPRA template, NTCER Version 2025-02)
- Verify MPN application submitted (at least 8 weeks prior to commencement)
- Confirm funding access is functional (PRODA/HPOS for AGPT and RGTS from S1 2026)

## Slide 7: Onboarding the Training Post - Funding Access Setup

### Critical Funding Systems to Verify

**For AGPT Registrars:**
- Practice Manager has active **PRODA credentials**
- PRODA account linked to training post's **Organisation Site ID**
- **HPOS access is functional** (test and confirm)
- Required for:
  - National Consistent Payments (NCP) during CGT
  - Flexible Funds during AST

**For RGTS Registrars (From Semester 1, 2026):**

**CRUCIAL FUNDING UPDATE:**

From Semester 1, 2026, RGTS registrars access GP Training Incentive Payments via PRODA/HPOS (same process as AGPT). The TPA must ensure RGTS posts (from 2026 onwards) also have active PRODA/HPOS access, aligning them with AGPT practices.

**Action for TPA:**
- Confirm Practice Manager has:
  - Active PRODA credentials
  - HPOS access linked to Organisation Site ID
  - Understanding of GP Training Incentive Payments claims process

**Note:** Bank account details in RGHub Training Post Profile are no longer required for RGTS funding from Semester 1, 2026.

## Slide 9: Preparing for Commencement - Employment Contracts

### Mandatory Employment Contract Requirements

**All AGPT and RGTS registrars must be employed under:**
- **GPSA/GPRA employment contract templates**
- Contracts compliant with **NTCER Version 2025-02** (effective from pay cycle following 1 July 2025)

**Key Provisions to Verify:**
- **12% Superannuation Guarantee** (from 1 July 2025, paid with salary from 1 July 2026)
- **Monthly minimum billing cycle** (no longer fortnightly or weekly)
- **3.5% staged increase for CGT1 registrars** (commencing Semester 1, 2026)
- Mandated formal teaching time (3 hours/week GPT1, 1.5 hours/week GPT2)
- Educational release time (16 days per 12 months)

**For Aboriginal Medical Services (AMS):**
- AMS employing AGPT registrars must provide **copy of employment agreement to TPA before registrar commences**
- Required to enable AMS Salary Support payment processing

**Resource Links:**
- GPSA Employment Contracts: [https://gpsa.org.au/training-practice-menu/employment-support/](https://gpsa.org.au/training-practice-menu/employment-support/)
- NTCER 2025-02: [https://gpra.org.au/ntcer/](https://gpra.org.au/ntcer/)

## Slide 10: Medicare Provider Number (MPN) Process

**Timeline:**
- Registrar should apply for MPN **at least 8 weeks before placement commences**
- Processing time: 4-6 weeks
- College must endorse MPN application before Medicare processes it

**TPA Role:**
- Confirm registrar has submitted MPN application via MyCollege Dashboard
- Follow up if MPN not issued by commencement date
- **Multi-site placements:** Confirm separate MPN applications submitted for each accredited location

**Critical:**
- Patients cannot claim Medicare rebate if registrar does not have active MPN
- Registrar should not commence billing until MPN is active

## Slide 11: Initial Weeks - Reporting Requirements

### ACRRM Reporting Schedule

**Week 5: TPA CGT Pulse Check**
- Confirmation registrar settling well
- Orientation complete (practice orientation and community integration)
- Teaching schedule being followed
- Supervision arrangements working

**Week 11: Mid-Semester Training Practice Report**
- Practice Manager completes and submits report via Microsoft Forms link (sent by ACRRM EDM)
- Supervisor conducts MiniCEX and/or CBD formative assessments

**Week 22 onwards: End-of-Semester Reports**
- Practice Manager completes End-of-Semester Training Practice Report (via MS Forms link)
- Principal Supervisor completes Supervisor Report (currently paper-based)

**TPA Action:**
- Remind Practice Managers of upcoming reporting deadlines at Week 5 check-in
- Follow up if reports not received by due dates

## Slide 12: Mid-Semester and End-of-Semester Reporting

**Training Practice Report Content:**
- Registrar attendance and engagement
- Teaching and supervision quality
- Clinical experience and patient demographics
- Assessments completed (MiniCEX, CBD, DOPS)
- Registrar performance and progress
- Support needs or concerns

**Supervisor Report:**
- Detailed assessment of registrar's clinical competence
- Consultation skills and clinical reasoning
- Professional behaviours and progress towards Fellowship
- Currently paper-based (electronic submission under development)

**TPA Role:**
- Ensure Practice Manager understands reporting requirements
- Assist Practice Manager to support supervisor with Supervisor Report completion
- Follow up if reports not received by deadlines

## Slide 14: Ongoing Support - Policy and Funding Updates

### 1. GP Training Incentive Payments (Effective Semester 1, 2026)

**New Commonwealth Funding for RGTS Registrars:**

From Semester 1, 2026, RGTS registrars receive GP Training Incentive Payments:

1. **Salary Support:** $30,000 per registrar per annum (paid to training post/employer)
2. **Study Leave Support:** 5 paid study leave days per year (in addition to educational release time)
3. **Parental Leave Support:** Up to 20 weeks paid parental leave for primary caregivers

**TPA Action:**
- Ensure Practice Managers understand PRODA/HPOS claims process
- Confirm Practice Managers monitor quarterly payment cycles
- Assist with troubleshooting if payments delayed or incorrect

## Slide 15: Ongoing Support - NTCER and Flexible Funds Updates

### 2. NTCER Version 2025-02 Key Changes

**Critical Updates for Practice Managers:**

- **Superannuation Guarantee:** 12% from 1 July 2025 (payment integrated with salary from 1 July 2026)
- **Monthly Billing Cycle:** Minimum billing cycle must be monthly (no longer fortnightly or weekly)
- **CGT1 Base Rate Increase:** 3.5% staged increase for first-term registrars commencing Semester 1, 2026

**TPA Action:**
- Confirm practices have updated employment contracts to reflect NTCER 2025-02
- Verify billing systems updated to monthly cycle
- Provide GPSA/GPRA resources if Practice Manager needs employment guidance

---

### 3. Flexible Funds for AGPT AST Registrars

**Quarterly Payment Structure:**

- Flexible Funds are **quarterly payments to training posts** for clinical teaching during AST terms
- Require correct PRODA/HPOS setup and NCP framework compliance
- Practice Managers should **confirm payment receipt each quarter** and report discrepancies to TPA

**TPA Action:**
- Confirm Practice Manager monitors quarterly Flexible Funds payment cycles
- Assist with troubleshooting if payments delayed or incorrect
- Escalate to ACRRM Finance Team if persistent payment issues

## Slide 17: Troubleshooting and Escalation

**Common Issues TPAs May Encounter:**

- MPN application delays or issues
- Funding access problems (PRODA/HPOS setup, payment delays)
- Employment contract non-compliance with NTCER
- Reporting delays or non-submission
- Teaching time not quarantined in roster
- Supervision arrangement concerns
- Registrar or supervisor concerns about workplace issues

**When to Escalate:**
- **ACRRM Finance Team:** Persistent funding payment issues
- **Post Accreditation Team:** Accreditation compliance concerns
- **Medical Educator:** Registrar progression concerns, educational issues
- **National AST Coordinator:** AST-specific clinical or educational queries
- **Regional Director of Training:** Serious concerns affecting registrar safety or training quality

## Slide 21: Systems - Aptify

**Aptify - This is where the majority of your TPA activity happens**

**Key Functions:**
- Training Post contact management
- Placement documentation tracking
- Communication logs and history
- Organisation Site ID recording (critical for PRODA/HPOS funding)

### Mandatory Aptify Contact Logging

**Each contact with the Training Post and/or Supervisor must be recorded in Aptify as a Contact Log entry.**

This ensures:
- Continuity of support and quality assurance
- Other TPAs can understand post's history and support needs if TPA changes
- ACRRM can monitor training post engagement and identify support needs

**Best Practice:**
- Log contact immediately after each interaction (phone call, email, site visit, check-in)
- Include sufficient detail for another TPA to understand context
- Use consistent categories and tags for easy searching

## Slide 23: PRODA/HPOS - Government Funding Access

**PRODA (Provider Digital Access):**
- Australian Government authentication system
- Practice Managers need PRODA credentials to access HPOS

**HPOS (Health Professional Online Services):**
- Government portal for NCP claims (AGPT registrars during CGT)
- Flexible Funds claims (AGPT registrars during AST)
- **GP Training Incentive Payments claims (RGTS registrars from Semester 1, 2026)**

**TPA Role:**
- Confirm Practice Manager has active PRODA/HPOS access
- Verify PRODA account linked to training post's Organisation Site ID
- Provide setup guidance if needed (or escalate to ACRRM Finance Team)

**Critical:**
- PRODA/HPOS access must be functional **before registrar commences** to avoid funding payment delays

## Slide 24: Summary - Your Role as TPA

**You are the College's point of contact for Training Posts and Supervisors.**

**Your key responsibilities:**
1. **Onboard training posts** and ensure funding access setup
2. **Verify compliance** with employment contracts (GPSA/GPRA templates, NTCER 2025-02)
3. **Conduct regular check-ins** (Week 5, ongoing support throughout semester)
4. **Monitor reporting** and follow up if reports not submitted
5. **Provide policy updates** (GP Training Incentive Payments, NTCER changes, Flexible Funds)
6. **Build strong relationships** with Practice Managers and supervisors
7. **Escalate concerns** appropriately to Medical Educators, RDOTs, or ACRRM teams
8. **Document all contacts** in Aptify as Contact Log entries

**Remember:**
- Some training posts will need minimal support; others will need intensive guidance
- Proactive, regular contact builds trust and enables early identification of issues
- Your role is critical to ensuring registrars have safe, supportive, high-quality training environments
