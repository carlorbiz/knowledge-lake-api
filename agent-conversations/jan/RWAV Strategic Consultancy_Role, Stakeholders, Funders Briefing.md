BRIEFING PAPER: RWAV Strategic Consultancy
Executive Summary
This briefing paper provides a comprehensive overview of Rural Workforce Agency Victoria (RWAV) to inform your upcoming 12-week strategic consultancy. RWAV is a significant non-profit organisation established in 1997, dedicated to improving access to quality primary healthcare services for rural, regional, and First Nations communities across Victoria. The organisation operates through government funding to deliver workforce solutions, grants, outreach services, recruitment, and data-driven advocacy to address healthcare workforce shortages in underserved areas.

Organisational Overview
Role and Purpose
RWAV's primary purpose is "providing effective and customised health workforce solutions for rural and First Nations communities in Victoria" with a vision that "all Victorian communities be supported with equitable access to a high-quality health workforce that promotes positive health outcomes."

The organisation fulfils three core functions:

Providing health workforce recruitment, locum services, outreach and grant programs
Influencing key decision-makers through health workforce data, research and analysis
Collaborating with stakeholders to support a well-distributed, high-quality health workforce throughout Victoria
Governance Structure
RWAV is governed by a skills-based Board of Directors with extensive experience in healthcare, governance, policy, and management. The Board operates through several committees:

Strategy, Policy and Stakeholder Engagement Committee
Governance Committee
Audit and Risk Committee
People and Culture Committee
Nominations and Remuneration Committee
The organisation is led by Chief Executive Officer Lauren Cordwell, who has been with RWAV since 2018 and brings over 20 years of experience in designing and implementing healthcare programs.

Key Programs and Services
1. Health Professional Recruitment
RWAV works directly with rural and regional medical practices to address healthcare workforce shortages by recruiting:

Registered clinicians
Nurses
Allied health professionals
Medical specialists
Practice managers
The organisation manages dedicated recruitment programs including the More Doctors for Rural Australia and 3GA programs.

2. Grants and Scholarships
RWAV administers various grants and scholarships aimed at:

Supporting health professionals' relocation to rural areas
Funding professional development and postgraduate education
Encouraging student placements in rural settings
Supporting rural practice infrastructure and sustainability
A key initiative is the Health Workforce Scholarship Program (HWSP), which provides funding for clinical upskilling for health professionals in rural and regional Victoria.

3. Outreach Services
RWAV manages seven Commonwealth Government-funded outreach programs delivering over 400 essential health services to rural, regional, and Aboriginal communities:

Rural Health Outreach Fund
Medical Outreach Indigenous Chronic Disease Program
Healthy Ears Better Listening Better Hearing Program
Visiting Optometrists Scheme
Eye and Ear Surgical Support
Coordination of Indigenous Eye Health
Ear Health Coordination
These programs focus on increasing access to maternal and paediatric care, mental health support, chronic disease management, and ear and eye health services.

4. Workforce Data and Analysis
RWAV collects and manages data to improve health outcomes in rural, regional and Aboriginal communities. This evidence-based approach informs policy recommendations and program design to address workforce distribution challenges.

5. Workforce Support Programs
The organisation administers programs supporting non-vocationally recognised doctors to gain valuable general practice experience in rural and remote Victorian communities, including telehealth initiatives.

6. Future Workforce Development
RWAV's Future Workforce program focuses on:

Raising awareness of rural health careers
Assisting with student placements
Providing grants and bursaries for university entrance exams and placements
Funding and Financial Structure
RWAV is a non-profit organisation primarily funded through:

Federal Government - Department of Health and Aged Care

Workforce Incentive Program (WIP)
Health Workforce Scholarship Program (HWSP)
Rural Health Outreach Fund and other outreach programs
State Government - Victorian Department of Health

Continuing Professional Development (CPD) support
Training programs for general practitioners
Regional mental health workforce incentives
As a government-funded organisation, RWAV operates within the constraints and reporting requirements of its funding agreements while maintaining its non-profit status.
