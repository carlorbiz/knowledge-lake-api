Types of Sentences
Understanding the different structures and purposes of sentences

Declarative
✓
Statements that provide information or express opinions
Examples: statement, fact, opinion, news, report
The library closes at nine o'clock every evening.
Interrogative
✓
Questions that seek information or require responses
Examples: question, inquiry, request, poll, survey
When did you finish your homework assignment?
Imperative
✓
Commands or requests that tell someone to do something
Examples: command, order, request, instruction, direction
Please turn in your papers before leaving class.
Exclamatory
✓
Expressions that show strong emotion or surprise
Examples: excitement, surprise, joy, anger, amazement
What an incredible performance that was!
Compound
✓
Two independent clauses joined by coordinating conjunctions
Examples: and, but, or, so, yet, for, nor
The weather was perfect, and we decided to have a picnic.
Complex
✓
One independent clause with one or more dependent clauses
Examples: because, since, when, if, although, while, unless
Although the test was difficult, most students passed with good grades.
Simple
✓
Contains one independent clause with subject and predicate
Examples: basic, direct, clear, straightforward, concise
Students study hard for their final exams.
Conditional
✓
Expresses hypothetical situations or consequences
Examples: if, unless, provided, suppose, imagine
If you study regularly, you will improve your grades significantly.
